push-ups

How to Do Push-Ups

  • Start in a plank position with your hands just wider than shoulder-width apart.
  • Extend your legs so they form a straight line with your back.
  • Lower yourself to the floor until your elbows are at 90 degrees.
  • Push yourself back up until your arms are straight.
  • Keep your core engaged, and maintain a straight line from head to heels throughout the movement.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Arching Your Back: Keep your body in a straight line. Avoid arching your back or letting your hips sag.
  • Elbow Lock: Donâ€™t fully lock your elbows at the top; maintain a slight bend.
  • Head Position: Look straight down to keep your neck aligned with your spine.
  • Wrist Protection: Keep your hands firmly rooted on the ground or on a mat to protect your wrists.

Progression Tips

  • Knee Push-Ups: If standard push-ups are challenging, start with knee push-ups.
  • Consistency: Practice consistently to build strength and improve technique.
  • Gradual Increase: Increase your reps gradually over time.
  • Core Strength: Strengthen your core to maintain stability during push-ups.
  • Full Range of Motion: Lower your chest close to the ground and fully extend your arms.

squats

How to Do Squats

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart or slightly wider, toes pointing slightly outward.
  • Keep your back straight, chest up, and gaze forward.
  • Engage your core muscles by pulling your belly button toward your spine.
  • Lower your body by bending your knees and pushing your hips back as if you're sitting back into a chair.
  • Lower yourself until your thighs are parallel to the ground or as far as your flexibility allows.
  • Keep your knees aligned with your toes, and make sure they don't extend beyond your toes.
  • Press through your heels to return to the starting position, straightening your legs and squeezing your glutes at the top.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Knees Over Toes: Avoid letting your knees extend beyond your toes as it can strain your knees.
  • Rounded Back: Keep your spine straight throughout the movement to avoid lower back strain.
  • Collapsing Knees: Ensure your knees stay in line with your toes and don't collapse inward.
  • Shallow Squats: Aim to squat until your thighs are parallel to the ground or lower for better activation of muscles.

Tips for Better Squats

  • Start Slow: Begin with bodyweight squats to master the form before adding weights.
  • Focus on Depth: Aim for proper depth rather than speed or quantity.
  • Use a Mirror: Watch yourself in a mirror to check your form and make adjustments as needed.
  • Engage Your Core: Keep your core muscles tight throughout the movement for stability and control.
  • Breathe: Inhale as you lower yourself into the squat and exhale as you push back up.

Plank

How to Do the Plank

  • Start by getting into a push-up position with your hands directly under your shoulders.
  • Engage your core muscles and keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
  • Hold this position, keeping your abs and glutes tight, and avoid sagging or arching your back.
  • Focus on breathing deeply and evenly throughout the exercise.
  • Hold the plank position for as long as you can, aiming for at least 30 seconds to start.

Tips for Better Planks

  • Keep your neck in line with your spine by looking down at the floor.
  • Avoid holding your breath; breathe normally throughout the exercise.
  • Engage your glutes and thigh muscles to help maintain stability.
  • If you feel any discomfort in your lower back, modify the plank by dropping to your knees.
  • As you build strength, challenge yourself by increasing the duration of your plank holds.

Benefits of Plank Workout

  • Strengthens your core muscles, including the abdominals, obliques, and lower back.
  • Improves overall stability and balance.
  • Helps alleviate lower back pain by strengthening the muscles that support the spine.
  • Requires no equipment and can be done anywhere, making it a convenient exercise option.

Lungs

How to Do Lunges

  • Start by standing tall with your feet hip-width apart.
  • Take a big step forward with one leg and lower your body until both knees are bent at about a 90-degree angle.
  • Make sure your front knee is directly above your ankle and your back knee is hovering just above the ground.
  • Keep your torso upright, core engaged, and shoulders relaxed.
  • Push back up to the starting position by driving through your front heel.
  • Repeat on the other leg.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Knee Alignment: Ensure your front knee stays aligned with your ankle and doesn't extend past your toes.
  • Leaning Forward: Keep your torso upright throughout the movement to prevent strain on your lower back.
  • Collapsing Knee: Avoid letting your back knee touch the ground; maintain a slight hover.
  • Too Wide or Narrow Stance: Maintain a hip-width distance between your feet for proper balance and stability.

Progression Tips

  • Weighted Lunges: Once comfortable with bodyweight lunges, add resistance using dumbbells or a barbell.
  • Walking Lunges: Incorporate forward-stepping lunges to increase the challenge and engage more muscles.
  • Pulse Lunges: Add pulses at the bottom of the lunge position to increase intensity and burn.
  • Reverse Lunges: Step backward instead of forward to target different muscle groups and improve balance.

Gult bridges

How to Do Glute Bridges

  • Start by lying on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the ground, hip-width apart.
  • Engage your core muscles by pulling your belly button toward your spine.
  • Press through your heels and lift your hips off the ground until your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees.
  • Squeeze your glutes at the top of the movement and hold for a second or two.
  • Lower your hips back down to the starting position with control.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Inactive Glutes: Make sure to actively squeeze your glutes throughout the movement.
  • Arching Your Back: Keep your spine neutral and avoid overarching your lower back.
  • Knees Caving In: Keep your knees in line with your hips and ankles, avoiding them from collapsing inward.
  • Not Fully Extending: Aim to fully extend your hips at the top of the movement for maximum glute activation.

Progression Tips

  • Single-Leg Glute Bridges: Challenge your balance and strength by performing glute bridges on one leg.
  • Weighted Glute Bridges: Increase resistance by placing a weight on your hips or using a resistance band.
  • Elevated Glute Bridges: Elevate your feet on a bench or platform to increase the range of motion and difficulty.
  • Pause at the Top: Hold the top position for a few seconds to increase time under tension and enhance muscle activation.

Jumping Jacks

How to Do Jumping Jacks

  • Start by standing with your feet together and your arms at your sides.
  • Jump up explosively while spreading your legs shoulder-width apart and simultaneously raising your arms above your head until your hands meet.
  • Land softly on the balls of your feet while bringing your legs back together and lowering your arms to your sides.
  • Repeat the movement continuously at a steady pace.

Benefits of Jumping Jacks

  • Cardiovascular Exercise: Jumping jacks increase your heart rate, making them an effective cardiovascular workout.
  • Full-Body Workout: They engage multiple muscle groups, including the legs, arms, core, and shoulders.
  • Calorie Burning: Jumping jacks are an excellent calorie-burning exercise that can aid in weight loss.
  • Improved Coordination: Performing jumping jacks regularly can enhance coordination and agility.

Tips for Performing Jumping Jacks

  • Land softly to reduce impact on your joints and minimize the risk of injury.
  • Maintain a steady pace throughout the exercise to maximize cardiovascular benefits.
  • Keep your core engaged and your back straight to support proper posture.
  • Breathe naturally and rhythmically as you perform jumping jacks.

Russian Twists

How to Do Russian Twists

  • Start by sitting on the floor with your knees bent and feet lifted off the ground, creating a V-shape with your torso and thighs.
  • Hold a weight or medicine ball with both hands close to your chest.
  • Lean back slightly to engage your core muscles.
  • Rotate your torso to the right, bringing the weight or ball towards the floor beside your hip.
  • Return to the center and then rotate your torso to the left, bringing the weight or ball towards the floor beside your left hip.
  • Continue alternating sides in a controlled motion, keeping your core engaged throughout.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Rounding Your Back: Keep your back straight throughout the movement to avoid strain.
  • Using Momentum: Avoid swinging the weight or ball with momentum; focus on controlled movements.
  • Not Engaging Core: Make sure to engage your core muscles throughout the exercise to maximize effectiveness.
  • Overarching: Avoid arching your back excessively; maintain a stable spine.

Progression Tips

  • Adding Resistance: Increase the intensity by using a heavier weight or medicine ball.
  • Increasing Reps: Gradually increase the number of reps as you build strength and endurance.
  • Advanced Variations: Try more challenging variations such as Russian twists with extended arms or elevated feet.
  • Combining with Other Exercises: Incorporate Russian twists into a full-body workout routine for comprehensive core strengthening.

High Knees

How to Do High Knees

  • Start by standing with your feet hip-width apart and arms by your sides.
  • Engage your core muscles and lift your right knee towards your chest as high as possible while simultaneously swinging your left arm forward.
  • Lower your right leg and return to the starting position.
  • Repeat the movement with your left knee, swinging your right arm forward this time.
  • Continue alternating knees in a running motion, bringing them up towards your chest as fast as possible.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Slouching Posture: Maintain an upright posture throughout the exercise to engage your core and maximize effectiveness.
  • Not Lifting Knees High Enough: Aim to bring your knees up as close to your chest as possible with each repetition.
  • Incorrect Arm Movement: Coordinate your arm movement with your leg movement, swinging them in sync to maintain balance and momentum.
  • Slow Pace: Perform the exercise at a fast pace to elevate your heart rate and increase intensity.

Progression Tips

  • Increasing Speed: Gradually increase the speed of your high knees to challenge your cardiovascular system and improve agility.
  • Adding Intervals: Incorporate high knees into interval training sessions, alternating between high-intensity bursts and recovery periods.
  • Adding Variations: Experiment with different variations such as high knees with high jumps or incorporating arm movements for additional challenge.
  • Combining with Other Exercises: Include high knees as part of a dynamic warm-up or cardio circuit for a full-body workout.

Tricep Dips

How to Do Triceps Dips

  • Start by sitting on a stable surface such as a bench or chair with your hands placed beside your hips, fingers pointing forward.
  • With your feet flat on the ground, slide your hips off the surface and walk your feet forward slightly.
  • Bend your elbows and lower your body until your upper arms are parallel to the ground, keeping your back close to the surface.
  • Push through your palms to straighten your arms and return to the starting position.
  • Keep your shoulders down and back throughout the movement, and avoid shrugging or hunching.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, focusing on controlled movements and maintaining proper form.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Flaring Elbows: Keep your elbows close to your body to target the triceps effectively and avoid strain on the shoulders.
  • Partial Range of Motion: Lower your body until your upper arms are parallel to the ground for full triceps activation.
  • Arching Back: Maintain a straight back throughout the movement to avoid strain on the lower back.
  • Using Momentum: Avoid swinging your body or using momentum to lift yourself up; focus on using your triceps strength.

Progression Tips

  • Add Resistance: Increase the difficulty by wearing a weighted vest or placing a weight plate on your lap.
  • Elevate Feet: Perform triceps dips with your feet elevated on a stable surface to increase the challenge.
  • Variations: Explore different variations such as bench dips or triceps dips using parallel bars for variety and muscle engagement.
  • Controlled Tempo: Focus on a slow and controlled tempo, emphasizing the eccentric (lowering) phase for greater muscle activation.

Workout Tips

Here are some tips on how to do the workouts properly:

    • Warm-Up: Always start with a proper warm-up to prepare your body for exercise and reduce the risk of injury.
    • Proper Form: Focus on maintaining proper form throughout the workout to maximize effectiveness and prevent injuries.
    • Breathe: Remember to breathe properly during exercises, inhaling on the eccentric phase and exhaling on the concentric phase.
    • Hydration: Stay hydrated before, during, and after your workout to optimize performance and recovery.
    • Listen to Your Body: Pay attention to your body's signals and adjust the intensity or technique as needed to avoid overexertion or injury.
